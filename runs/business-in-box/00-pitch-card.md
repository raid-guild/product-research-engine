# Pitch Card: Business-in-a-Box

## One-Sentence Pitch

Business-in-a-Box helps solo and small-crew handyman/home-repair operators get jobs, invoices, payment reminders, receipts, and accountant-ready records under control with concierge setup and a lightweight automated back office, so they can stop losing nights and weekends to scattered admin.

## Who It Is For

U.S. solo or 1-3 person handyman/home-repair operators who already have customers and revenue, but manage the business across texts, paper, spreadsheets, memory, payment apps, and tax-season cleanup.

## Pain Or Desire

They are not trying to buy another software tool. They want to stop missing follow-ups, chasing unpaid invoices manually, losing job notes and receipts, and scrambling to hand clean records to an accountant.

## Why Now

Small service businesses already pay for fragments of this workflow through field-service apps, accounting tools, payment processors, website builders, and manual bookkeeping help. AI and automation make it more plausible to package the messy middle into a guided, lower-learning back-office workflow.

## First Wedge

Admin recovery and ongoing operations for handyman/home-repair operators: quotes, jobs, invoices, payment reminders, review requests, receipt capture, and monthly records packets.

## Existing Alternatives

- Jobber, Housecall Pro, and other field-service tools.
- QuickBooks, Wave, Square, Stripe, Wix, HoneyBook, and spreadsheets.
- Manual admin work, bookkeepers, accountants, texts, paper, memory, and doing nothing until tax season.

## Interesting If True

Handyman operators may pay $149-$249/month if the product creates a visible first-week outcome: cleaner jobs, faster invoices, automated follow-up, and accountant-ready monthly records without requiring them to learn a full software suite.

## Biggest Unknown

Whether the buyer sees this as a must-have operations fix or dismisses it as "just another invoicing/admin tool."

## Internal Reaction Prompt

Do we believe handyman/home repair is the right first wedge, or should the same "digital operations department" idea be tested first with cleaning, landscaping, or another local service vertical?
