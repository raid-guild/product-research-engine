# GTM Test Plan

## GTM Risk

GTM is the highest-risk part of the idea. The founder can build the product, but the customer does not actively search for "AI operations department." They search for immediate fixes: invoicing, getting paid, organizing receipts, missed calls, reviews, QuickBooks help, and how to look professional.

The first GTM tests should validate direct pain and willingness to pay without paid ads as the primary engine.

## First 3 Channel Tests

### Test 1: Local Operator Direct Outreach

Target:

- Handyman/home-repair operators found through Google Maps, local Facebook groups, Nextdoor, Craigslist services, Thumbtack-like profiles, and local referrals.

Offer:

> "I am testing a done-with-you admin cleanup for handyman businesses: quotes, invoices, follow-ups, review requests, and accountant-ready monthly records. I will set it up with you this week. Looking for 10 operators."

Goal:

- 50 outreaches.
- 15 replies.
- 8 calls.
- 3 paid setup pilots.

Pass threshold:

- At least 2 paid pilots at $299-$499 within 14 days.

### Test 2: Accountant / Bookkeeper Referral Interviews

Target:

- Local bookkeepers and small-business accountants serving trades and home-service clients.

Offer:

> "I am building a pre-bookkeeping records organizer for small home-service operators so your clients hand over cleaner invoices, receipts, payments, and monthly summaries."

Goal:

- 25 partner conversations.
- 10 identify this as a real problem.
- 5 willing to refer one messy client or review a sample packet.

Pass threshold:

- 3 warm customer introductions or 2 partners willing to co-test.

### Test 3: Problem-Led Content + Landing Page

Channels:

- YouTube Shorts/TikTok/Reels.
- Local Facebook group posts where allowed.
- Reddit only where rules permit and without spam.
- SEO pages for concrete problems.

Angles:

- "Handyman invoice tracker."
- "How to stop forgetting unpaid invoices."
- "Monthly records packet for your handyman business."
- "What to send your accountant as a handyman."
- "Simple handyman job tracker."

Goal:

- 5 landing pages.
- 5 short demos.
- 100 targeted visits.
- 10 email captures.
- 3 calls.

Pass threshold:

- 5 percent visitor-to-lead conversion on pain-specific pages or 3 qualified calls from organic/community posts.

## Outreach Scripts

### Direct Operator Script

Subject: quick admin cleanup for your handyman business

Hi [Name],

I am testing a setup service for handyman/home-repair businesses that gets quotes, invoices, payment reminders, job notes, review requests, and accountant-ready records organized in one place.

The idea is simple: you do the work, and the admin stops living in texts, photos, and memory.

I am looking for 10 operators to run through a discounted setup. Would you be open to a 15-minute call this week to see if it would actually save you time?

### Bookkeeper Script

Subject: cleaner records from small trades clients

Hi [Name],

I am researching a lightweight records and operations system for small home-service operators before they hand things to a bookkeeper or CPA.

It would organize invoices, payments, receipts, job notes, and monthly summaries, but would not provide tax advice or file returns.

Do you see clients who show up with messy records from texts, payment apps, paper, and spreadsheets? I would value 15 minutes of feedback and can share a sample monthly packet.

### Facebook / Local Group Post

Testing something for handyman and home-repair folks:

I am looking for a few operators who have customers but feel buried in quotes, invoices, unpaid reminders, receipts, and tax-time records. I am offering a discounted admin cleanup setup in exchange for blunt feedback.

This is not tax/legal advice and not a big software platform. It is meant to get the boring business stuff organized so you can do the work.

Comment or DM if you want to see it.

## GTM Risk Assessment

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---:|---|
| Buyers do not search for the category | High | Sell pain-specific outcomes, not category language. |
| Founder-led outreach is uncomfortable | High | Use structured scripts, partner intros, and problem content. |
| Local groups reject promotion | Medium | Ask for feedback, follow group rules, avoid spam, offer useful templates. |
| Setup is too manual | High | Charge setup fee, cap scope, instrument repeated steps. |
| Operators want full Jobber replacement | Medium | Qualify for solo/simple workflows; refer complex teams away. |
| Partners want revenue share/service work | Medium | Start with referral fee or co-branded records packet, not white-glove bookkeeping. |

## Partner Channel Shortlist

1. Bookkeepers serving trades.
2. Solo CPA firms with messy small-business clients.
3. Formation providers only after product has operating retention.
4. Insurance agents serving contractors.
5. Local chambers and small business development centers.
6. Property managers who refer repair vendors.
