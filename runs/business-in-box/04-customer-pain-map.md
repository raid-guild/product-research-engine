# Customer Pain Map

Primary workflow: solo or small-crew handyman/home-repair operator.

| Workflow Stage | Current Behavior | Pain | Business Impact | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Customer inquiry | Calls, texts, Facebook messages, Google Business Profile, referrals. | Leads live everywhere; operator forgets context. | Missed jobs, slow response, lower close rate. | Must solve |
| Qualification | Back-and-forth texts and photos. | No consistent intake; wasting time on bad-fit jobs. | Unpaid estimating time, schedule churn. | Must solve |
| Estimate / quote | Texted ballpark, spreadsheet, Square invoice, paper, memory. | Hard to standardize scope and pricing. | Undercharging, disputes, rework. | Must solve |
| Scheduling | Phone calendar, texts, memory. | Double booking, no prep notes, missed appointments. | Lost trust and lost revenue. | Must solve |
| Job tracking | Photos in camera roll, receipts in truck, notes in texts. | Job evidence and costs not attached to work. | Disputes, weak records, inaccurate profitability. | Must solve |
| Invoicing | Sent late, manually, or not at all. | Invoice happens after a long day, then gets postponed. | Cash-flow delay. | Must solve |
| Payment collection | Cash, check, Zelle, Venmo, Square, unpaid invoice. | No automatic reminders or reconciliation. | A/R drag and awkward chasing. | Must solve |
| Customer follow-up | Usually skipped. | No review ask, referral ask, maintenance reminder. | Lower repeat/referred work. | Must solve |
| Review request | Manual text if remembered. | Inconsistent timing and language. | Weak local reputation engine. | Should solve |
| Repeat booking | Memory or seasonal texts. | No reactivation workflow. | Missed recurring revenue. | Should solve |
| Bookkeeping export | Receipts and invoices gathered at tax time. | Panic, missing documents, accountant asks questions. | Higher accountant cost, stress, possible deductions missed by user/accountant. | Must solve |
| Compliance reminders | State/local dates, insurance, licenses, sales tax if applicable. | Confusing and risky. | Penalties or unauthorized work risk. | Nice to have with careful boundaries |

## Pain Ranking

1. Getting paid late or forgetting to invoice.
2. Losing leads and follow-ups in texts.
3. Not knowing what is scheduled or promised.
4. Tax-season/CPA record scramble.
5. No consistent review/referral engine.
6. Underquoting because job details and costs are not tracked.
7. Switching between too many apps.
8. Fear of doing the wrong business/tax/legal thing.

## Must Solve

- Central job/customer timeline.
- Estimate/invoice creation.
- Payment status and reminders.
- Job notes/photos/receipts attached to the job.
- Review request after payment.
- Monthly accountant-ready packet export.
- Minimal onboarding that starts from texts/photos/spreadsheets rather than clean data.

## Nice To Have

- Website builder.
- Full bookkeeping ledger.
- Payroll.
- Inventory.
- Advanced dispatch.
- Route optimization.
- Legal entity formation.
- Tax filing.
- Deep CRM analytics.

## Willingness-To-Pay Triggers

Highest willingness:

- "I got paid faster."
- "I stopped forgetting follow-ups."
- "My accountant did not hate me this month."
- "I booked repeat work from old customers."
- "I can see every unpaid invoice and unfinished job."

Lower willingness:

- "AI helps me manage my business."
- "Everything in one dashboard."
- "Learn bookkeeping."
- "Start your LLC."

## Product Implication

The first product should feel like an operational cleanup service that becomes software. Do the first setup with the customer, then let automation keep the system current.
