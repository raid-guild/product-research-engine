# Continue, Narrow, Pivot, Or Kill Recommendation

## Recommendation

Continue, but narrow.

The broad opportunity is real, but the initial product must be much narrower than "business-in-a-box." Start with a digital operations department for existing handyman/home-repair operators.

## Decision

| Option | Verdict | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Continue broad Business-in-a-Box | No | Too crowded, episodic, and legally/tax-advice adjacent as a first wedge. |
| Narrow to Ops Kit for handyman/home repair | Yes | Best balance of pain, reachability, recurring workflow, manageable compliance, and plausible $149-$249/month pricing. |
| Pivot to formation-only | No | Formation competitors already offer low-cost entry and monetize with upsells. Recurring value is weaker. |
| Pivot to QuickBooks replacement | No for now | Too broad and high-trust. Better to feed QuickBooks/accountants clean records first. |
| Kill idea | No | Market size, competitor pricing, and buyer pain support a focused validation test. |

## Why Continue

- A specific vertical has urgent recurring operations pain.
- Customers already pay for inferior or fragmented solutions.
- The MVP can avoid regulated advice by organizing records and routing professional questions out.
- $149/month is plausible because incumbents validate higher software spend.
- The $10k/month checkpoint is reachable without hundreds of accounts.
- Concierge onboarding is acceptable if charged and capped.
- The first value event can happen within one week.

## Why Narrow

The current concept spans formation, banking, websites, payments, CRM, bookkeeping, marketing, compliance, and tax prep. That is too much surface area for a first product and too hard to message.

The winning narrow promise:

> "Get your handyman admin under control this week."

## Validation Gates

Proceed from research to pilot only if, within 30 days:

- 20 target operator interviews completed.
- 10 partner interviews completed.
- 3 paid setup pilots closed.
- At least 2 customers agree that $149/month is plausible after setup.
- At least 2 customers send invoices or payment reminders through the workflow.
- At least 2 customers generate a monthly records packet.
- Manual support after setup is under 60 minutes/customer/month.

Narrow further if:

- Handyman buyers are too inconsistent.
- Cleaning or landscaping shows stronger repeatable pain.
- Operators want only invoice/payment tools and not records.

Pivot if:

- Bookkeepers/accountants are the actual buyer.
- Operators will not pay but partners will pay for client cleanup.
- The strongest demand is "admin assistant service" rather than SaaS.

Kill if:

- No paid setup pilots after 50 qualified outreaches and 20 interviews.
- Most buyers refuse $99+/month.
- Regulated tax/legal advice becomes central to willingness to pay.
- Support demands exceed the economics even with setup fees.

## Final Call

Continue with a narrow, paid concierge pilot for handyman/home-repair operators:

- Offer: $499 setup plus $149/month.
- Goal: 10 pilots.
- Build only what repeats across pilots.
- Keep AI invisible and practical.
- Keep legal/tax boundaries explicit.

The business should not try to be a full SMB operating system yet. Earn the right to expand by solving the job-to-payment-to-records workflow first.
