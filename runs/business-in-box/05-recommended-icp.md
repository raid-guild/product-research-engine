# Recommended ICP

## Primary ICP

U.S.-based solo or small-crew handyman/home-repair operator with an active book of work and messy admin.

## Firmographics

- Geography: United States.
- Business type: handyman, home repair, light remodel support, maintenance, small odd-job operator.
- Team size: owner-operator, or owner plus 1-3 helpers.
- Revenue: roughly $8k-$50k/month.
- Business maturity: 1-7 years operating, not brand new.
- Customer mix: homeowners, landlords, property managers, real estate agents, small commercial spaces.
- Current systems: text messages, Google Calendar, Square/Stripe/Venmo/Zelle/checks, spreadsheets, paper, QuickBooks/Wave or an accountant at tax time.

## Buyer Profile

The buyer is usually the owner. They are field-first, not software-first.

They believe:

- Good work should generate referrals.
- Admin takes too much time.
- Software is annoying unless it directly saves time or gets them paid.
- They should be more organized, but they do not want to become a bookkeeper.

They dislike:

- Long setup.
- Complex accounting categories.
- "Book a demo" sales friction.
- Being forced into a big platform before they have a team.
- Vague AI language.

## Strong Buying Triggers

- Tax season was painful.
- A customer disputed scope or payment.
- They forgot to follow up and lost a job.
- They are hiring their first helper.
- They are getting more leads from Google/Facebook than they can manage.
- Their spouse, accountant, or office helper is pushing them to get organized.
- They want to look more professional without paying for a heavy field-service suite.

## Exclusions

Do not start with:

- Brand-new "idea stage" founders with no customers.
- Operators doing under $5k/month unless they pay a setup fee.
- Licensed HVAC/plumbing/electrical businesses expecting full field-service functionality.
- Multi-crew businesses needing dispatch, inventory, price books, service agreements, financing, and advanced reporting.
- Customers who expect tax filing, legal advice, entity recommendation, or bookkeeping judgment.

## Segment Expansion Path

1. Handyman/home repair.
2. Pressure washing/exterior cleaning.
3. Residential cleaning.
4. Landscaping/lawn care.
5. Licensed trades only after the product can handle deeper workflows or through partners.

## Buyer Message

Primary:

> "Get your handyman admin under control this week."

Secondary:

> "Quotes, invoices, reminders, review requests, job notes, receipts, and accountant-ready monthly reports, set up for you."

Avoid:

> "AI copilot for small business."

AI should be disclosed honestly when relevant, but it should not be the emotional hook.
