# Customer Interview Script

## Respondent Screener

Accept if:

- U.S.-based service business operator.
- Currently serving paying customers.
- Owner or decision-maker.
- Handles or oversees quotes, scheduling, invoices, payments, and records.
- For primary interviews: handyman/home repair or close adjacent vertical.

Reject or deprioritize if:

- No paying customers yet.
- Already runs a mature field-service platform successfully.
- Only wants legal/tax advice.
- Not involved in admin or buying decisions.

## Interview Goals

- Identify strongest buyer segment.
- Rank top operating pains.
- Learn current alternatives and workarounds.
- Test willingness to pay.
- Validate messages.
- Identify channel behavior.
- Detect legal/tax expectation risk.

## Opening

Thanks for talking with me. I am researching how very small service businesses manage the admin side: quotes, scheduling, invoices, payment follow-up, customer records, and accountant-ready records. I am not selling anything on this call, and I am not asking for legal or tax information. I am trying to understand what is painful enough to pay for.

## Business Context

1. What kind of service business do you run?
2. How long have you been operating?
3. Is this full-time or part-time?
4. Roughly how many jobs do you complete in a typical week or month?
5. Do you work solo or with helpers/crew?
6. Where do most customers come from?

## Current Workflow

1. Walk me through what happens from first customer inquiry to getting paid.
2. Where do inquiries come in?
3. How do you create quotes or estimates?
4. How do you schedule jobs?
5. Where do you keep job notes, photos, and customer details?
6. How do you send invoices?
7. How do customers pay?
8. What happens when someone does not pay right away?
9. Do you ask for reviews or referrals?
10. What do you send your accountant or tax preparer?

## Pain Discovery

1. What part of the admin work do you hate most?
2. What has actually cost you money?
3. What do you keep meaning to organize but never do?
4. When was the last time this caused a real problem?
5. If you could remove one admin task from your week, what would it be?
6. What do you do on nights/weekends that feels like unpaid office work?

## Current Tools

1. What software or apps do you currently use?
2. What do you use spreadsheets/paper/texts for?
3. Have you tried QuickBooks, Jobber, Housecall Pro, Square, Wave, HoneyBook, or anything similar?
4. What did you like?
5. What made it annoying or not worth it?
6. What do you currently pay for business software?

## Message Testing

Ask for reaction, not agreement:

1. "Get your handyman admin under control this week."
2. "Your back office without hiring an office manager."
3. "Quotes, invoices, reminders, review requests, and accountant-ready records, set up for you."
4. "Stop losing jobs and payments in texts."
5. "Get accountant-ready reports without learning bookkeeping software."

For each:

- What does this make you think it does?
- Is it believable?
- Is it interesting?
- What sounds wrong or too vague?

## Pricing Test

1. If someone set this up with you and cleaned up your current workflow, would that be valuable?
2. Would a one-time setup fee feel reasonable? At what price?
3. Would you rather pay monthly, one-time, or both?
4. If it saved 2-4 hours/month and helped invoices get paid faster, what monthly price would be fair?
5. What would have to be included for $149/month to feel worth it?
6. What would make $249/month worth it?
7. At what price would you expect too much hands-on service?

## Buying Trigger

1. What would make you buy this now rather than "someday"?
2. Who else would need to approve?
3. Would your accountant/bookkeeper influence the decision?
4. Where would you look for a solution?
5. Who do you trust for software recommendations?

## Compliance Boundary Probe

1. Would you expect this product to tell you which entity to form?
2. Would you expect it to tell you what tax deductions to take?
3. Would organizing receipts, invoices, and summaries for your accountant be enough?
4. When would you prefer to be routed to a CPA, bookkeeper, attorney, or official source?

## Closing

If I offered a discounted setup where we organize your customers/jobs, invoice workflow, payment reminders, review requests, and first monthly records packet, would you want to try it?

If yes:

- What would you want set up first?
- What would you pay for that pilot?
- Can we schedule a follow-up?

## Validation Scorecard

| Signal | Strong | Medium | Weak |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pain urgency | Recent costly incident | Annoying recurring task | Abstract frustration |
| Current spend | Pays $50+/mo already | Pays some tools | Pays nothing and resists |
| Workflow frequency | Weekly/daily | Monthly | Occasional |
| Buying trigger | Has deadline/problem now | Wants improvement | Curious only |
| Price acceptance | $149+/mo plausible | $49-$99/mo | Only free/cheap |
| Setup willingness | Will pay setup | Would do free setup | Refuses onboarding |
| Boundary fit | Accepts records-only | Some advice expectation | Expects legal/tax advice |
