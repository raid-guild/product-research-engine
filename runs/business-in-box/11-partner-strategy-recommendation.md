# Partner Strategy Recommendation

## Partner Thesis

Partners are useful because trust is the hardest part of selling admin/records software to small operators. The best partners already see the mess: bookkeepers, CPAs, property managers, insurance agents, and formation providers.

Do not lead with formation-provider partnerships. They are crowded, affiliate-heavy, and likely to pull the product back toward one-time setup. Start with bookkeepers/accountants because the records packet is central to the product and creates mutual value.

## Priority Partner Types

| Priority | Partner | Why It Fits | First Ask |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Bookkeepers serving trades/home services | They suffer from messy client records and can refer operators before tax season. | Review sample monthly packet and introduce one messy client. |
| 2 | Small CPA firms | They have trust but limited patience for cleanup work. | Validate CPA-ready export and co-market a records cleanup offer. |
| 3 | Property managers | They know reliable repair vendors and need responsive, documented work. | Refer top handyman vendors who need admin help. |
| 4 | Insurance agents serving contractors | They touch operators at renewal and startup moments. | Cross-refer operators needing documentation and professionalism. |
| 5 | Local chambers / SBDCs | They host small business education and can provide credibility. | Run a workshop on "getting service-business records organized." |
| 6 | Formation providers | They own new-business setup flow. | Later affiliate or integration after operating retention is proven. |
| 7 | Banks/fintechs | They own financial account setup. | Later integration; not first acquisition channel. |

## Partner Offer

For bookkeepers/CPAs:

> "We help your small home-service clients send you cleaner monthly records: invoices, payments, receipts, job notes, and uncategorized items in one packet."

Partner value:

- Less cleanup.
- Better client retention.
- Potential referral fee.
- Clear boundary: product organizes records; partner gives advice.

## Partner Models

Start simple:

- Warm-introduction referral: $100-$250 referral fee after paid setup.
- Co-branded records packet review.
- Educational webinar.
- Partner directory listing.

Avoid early:

- Revenue-share complexity.
- White-label builds.
- Custom partner dashboards.
- Becoming a bookkeeping labor subcontractor.

## Sample Partner Outreach

Subject: cleaner monthly records from small trades clients

Hi [Name],

I am building a lightweight operations and records system for solo/small-crew home-service operators. The goal is to help them keep quotes, invoices, payments, receipts, job notes, and monthly summaries organized before they hand things to a bookkeeper or CPA.

It does not provide tax advice or file returns. It creates cleaner records and flags missing items.

Do you see clients who show up with scattered texts, receipts, payment apps, and incomplete invoice records? I would value 15 minutes of feedback on a sample monthly packet.

## First 30-Day Partner Test

1. Build sample monthly packet.
2. Interview 10 bookkeepers and 5 CPAs.
3. Ask each to identify the top 5 missing record problems.
4. Ask for one client intro only after they validate the packet.
5. Offer referral fee after a customer pays setup.

Pass criteria:

- 8 of 15 partners say the packet solves a real recurring problem.
- 3 partners provide warm introductions.
- 2 referred operators pay for setup.

## Long-Term Partner Direction

Build toward:

- CPA/bookkeeper export formats.
- Partner-reviewed checklist templates.
- Accountant portal for read-only packet access.
- Referral marketplace.
- Official-source compliance reminders.

Keep the product boundary:

- Partner gives advice.
- Product organizes, tracks, reminds, and exports.
