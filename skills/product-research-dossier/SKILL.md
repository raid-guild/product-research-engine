# Product Research Dossier

## Overview

Turn a signaled product idea into a decision-oriented research dossier. Build from the pitch artifacts, preserve uncertainties, and produce files that help decide whether to continue, narrow, pivot, or kill.

When writing files in this repository, use the existing idea run directory under `exploration/product-ideation-engine/runs/<idea-slug>/`.

## Inputs

Start with whatever exists:

- pitch card
- one-page product brief
- raw idea notes
- signal notes
- target geography or market
- known constraints, founder strengths, and weaknesses

If inputs are missing, make reasonable assumptions and mark them as assumptions. Ask only when a missing input would materially change the research scope.

## Workflow

1. Create or update `signal-notes.md`.
2. Create `research-plan.md` from `templates/research-plan.md`.
3. Research current market facts, pricing, competitors, and regulatory constraints using primary or credible sources.
4. Produce the dossier files in numbered order.
5. End with a clear continue, narrow, pivot, or kill recommendation.

## Research Rules

Use sources for any current market, pricing, law, competitor, or economic claim. Include source links in the relevant document.

Separate:

- evidence from inference
- buyer pain from founder intuition
- market size from reachable segment
- product promise from implementation details

Prefer narrow wedges over broad categories. Name what should not be built or researched yet.

## Dossier Files

Create these by default:

1. `00-product-intro-brief.md`
2. `01-market-opportunity-memo.md`
3. `02-segment-ranking-scorecard.md`
4. `03-competitor-pricing-matrix.md`
5. `04-customer-pain-map.md`
6. `05-recommended-icp.md`
7. `06-mvp-package-recommendation.md`
8. `07-gtm-test-plan.md`
9. `08-customer-interview-script.md`
10. `09-risk-boundary-notes.md`
11. `10-support-automation-opportunities.md`
12. `11-partner-strategy-recommendation.md`
13. `12-continue-narrow-pivot-kill-recommendation.md`

Optional follow-on files may include lead sourcing pilots, service-offering explorations, or experiment trackers when they are directly useful.

## Quality Gate

A dossier is ready when it contains:

- a specific recommended wedge
- the most credible buyer/user segment
- known alternatives and pricing bands
- validation questions and scripts
- risks and boundaries
- first offer or MVP package
- a decision recommendation with measurable gates

If the evidence is thin, say so directly and recommend the next cheapest signal-gathering step.
